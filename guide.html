🎮 Voidling Bound Beginner Guide: Getting Started

📅 Updated: June 8, 2026 ⏱️ 10 min read 🏷️ New Player

📑 On This Page

    1. What Is Voidling Bound?

    Voidling Bound is a third-person shooter meets monster tamer from Hatchery Games. You play as a Space Wrangler who uses neural-link tech to directly control alien creatures called Voidlings. Your mission: fight back against a parasitic corruption threatening all life across the galaxy.

    Think "Pokémon meets Mass Effect" — but you control the creatures directly in fast-paced action combat instead of turn-based battles.

    2. Choosing Your Difficulty

    There are four difficulty levels. Beginners should start on Collector or Adventurer:

    DifficultyNameRecommended For
    🟢 EasyCollectorFirst-time players, want to focus on collecting
    🟡 MediumAdventurerBalanced experience, some challenge
    🟠 HardBattlerExperienced action game players
    🔴 ExpertBeast TamerMaximum challenge, all mechanics required

    💡 Pro Tip

    Start on Adventurer. You can change difficulty later. Collector is too easy if you have any action game experience, and you'll miss learning important combat mechanics.

    3. Your First Voidlings

    At the start of the game, you choose between starter Voidlings:

    StarterStyleStrengthsBest For
    InfernautRanged DPSFlame Burst, fire AoE, burn effectsBeginners who prefer ranged firepower
    GelikMelee + CCFrost Slam, ice AoE, freeze buildupAggressive players, crowd control

    💡 Recommendation

    Pick Infernaut as your first. The ranged fire playstyle is safer while you learn enemy patterns. You can catch a melee Voidling later.

    4. Understanding the 5 Core Attributes

    When you level up, you get points to distribute across five stats:

    AttributeWhat It DoesPriority
    💪 StrengthIncreases BOTH melee and ranged attack damage⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    ❤️ VitalityMore HP, better survivability⭐⭐⭐⭐
    ✨ EssenceBoosts elemental damage (fire, ice, etc.) as % increase⭐⭐⭐
    🔄 RecuperationHealth recovery speed and effectiveness⭐⭐
    ⚡ AgilityMovement speed, dodge distance, attack speed⭐⭐⭐

    ⚠️ Common Mistake

    Don't spread points evenly! Focus on Strength first (damage is king), then Vitality for survival. Essence only matters if you use elemental-focused Voidlings.

    5. Combat Basics: What You Need to Know

    Combat in Voidling Bound is real-time third-person action. You directly control your Voidling:

    • Primary Attack: Each Voidling has a unique basic attack (shoot, slash, smash, or blast)
    • Secondary Skill: Special ability with cooldown — can be elemental or utility
    • Ultimate: Charged up over time, devastating AoE damage
    • Dodge: Essential! Invincibility frames during dodge rolls
    • Block / Parry: Some Voidlings can block; timing a perfect parry stuns enemies
    • Sprint & Dash: Use for repositioning and escaping danger zones

    💡 Pro Tip

    Learn enemy attack patterns first, then attack during their recovery windows. Spamming attacks without dodging gets you killed fast on higher difficulties.

    6. Your Ship Hub: Key Facilities

    As you progress, your ship unlocks critical facilities:

    • Breeding Station: Cross-breed two Voidlings to produce offspring with inherited stats and traits
    • Training Room: Assign Voidlings to passively level up while you use others on missions
    • Splicing Station: Late-game facility — mix genes from different species to create custom Voidlings

    7. First 2 Hours: Priority Checklist

    1. ✅ Pick Infernaut as your starter
    2. ✅ Set difficulty to Adventurer
    3. ✅ Complete the tutorial missions — they teach essential mechanics
    4. ✅ Put your first 5-10 attribute points into Strength
    5. ✅ Capture 3-4 different Voidlings to build your starter team
    6. ✅ Find your first Golden Egg (rare spawn with unique genes)
    7. ✅ Unlock the Training Room — start leveling backup Voidlings
    8. ✅ Unlock the Breeding Station — breed your best Voidlings
