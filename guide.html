🎮 Voidling Bound First Hour: What to Do

📅 Updated: June 10, 2026 ⏱️ 8 min read 🏷️ New Player

📑 On This Page

    ⚠️ Launch Week Notice

    Last verified: June 2026 launch build. Voidling Bound launched June 9, 2026. Attribute scaling, evolution requirements, and balance values may change with patches. Check in-game tooltips for current numbers.

    The Safe First Hour Route

    Your first hour in Voidling Bound should answer three questions: which Voidling feels good to control, how do the 5 attributes affect your playstyle, and what does the core loop (mission → evolve → repeat) feel like. Don't optimise yet — just learn.

    1. Play the free demo first (if you haven't) — it includes Kwipeck and Gilick, the two starter Voidlings. 30 minutes in the demo will answer more questions than any guide.
    2. Pick a starter that matches your playstyle — Kwipeck (long-range, minigun) or Gilick (close-range, shotgun). See the comparison below.
    3. Learn the controls and camera — third-person shooter fundamentals. You control the Voidling directly, not through menus.
    4. Complete the tutorial missions — they introduce combat, evolution, and the mission structure.
    5. Evolve once — get to your first evolution branch point and make a choice. See how the Voidling changes visually and mechanically.
    6. Then decide what to focus on — more Voidlings, deeper evolution, breeding, or pushing into Abyss.

    💡 Can't Decide?

    Pick Kwipeck if you prefer shooters. Pick Gilick if you prefer brawlers. Both are viable — the game has 9 base species and you will collect more. Your first choice does not lock you into one playstyle forever.

    Note: The free Steam demo displays this Voidling as "Glick." The official Hatchery Games Species Overview video (June 1, 2026) uses Gilick — that is the corrected, official name. Both refer to the same lizard starter.

    Controls — Third-Person Shooter Basics

    Voidling Bound is not turn-based. You control your Voidling directly in third-person, like Warframe or a character action game. Core controls:

    • Move — WASD or left stick (standard third-person movement)
    • Shoot / Primary attack — left mouse / right trigger
    • Secondary ability — varies by Voidling (bomb, leap, etc.)
    • Dodge / Dash — iframes-based evasion. Critical for survival.
    • Block / Parry — timing-based defense. Parry windows vary by Voidling.
    • Ultimate ability — charged ability, charges through combat actions
    • Sprint / Jump — standard movement tools

    Note: Exact keybinds may vary by platform and build. Check the in-game settings menu for your current bindings. Controller is supported and Steam Deck compatible.

    The 5 Attributes — What They Do

    Voidling Bound uses a 5-attribute system. Every Voidling has values in each attribute, and investing points (or inheriting via breeding) shapes your build.

    Attribute What It Affects Good For
    Strength Physical damage output Melee-focused Voidlings like Nimiod
    Vitality HP pool, defense, survivability Close-range brawlers like Gilick
    Essence Ability/special power, ultimate damage Ability-spam builds, elemental damage
    Recuperation Health regen, healing received Sustain builds, Abyss endurance runs
    Agility Movement speed, dodge cooldown, dash distance Hit-and-run builds like Kwipeck

    Note: Exact attribute scaling values (how much damage Strength adds per point, how much HP Vitality gives, etc.) are balance values that can change with patches. Check in-game tooltips for current numbers. The descriptions above are based on the official feature list and developer interviews.

    Difficulty Settings — What We Know

    Voidling Bound offers difficulty options. Based on the Steam page and developer descriptions:

    • Standard difficulty — balanced for the intended experience. Start here.
    • Harder difficulties — likely increase enemy HP/damage, reduce drop rates, or add modifiers. Exact tuning not yet documented.
    • Abyss mode — separate endless mode with its own escalating difficulty curve. Not a "difficulty setting" for the campaign.

    Note: Difficulty options and their exact effects will be confirmed as players progress through launch week. Check the in-game settings menu.

    First Missions — What to Expect

    The campaign opens with tutorial missions that teach core systems:

    1. Basic movement and combat — learn to control your starter Voidling
    2. First combat encounters — fight Bulbous or GenBot enemies (the two confirmed enemy factions)
    3. First evolution — reach a branch point and choose a path
    4. Mission structure — accept missions, deploy to planets (Vireo, Solum, Aulis), complete objectives, return for rewards

    Missions reward XP, credits, and sometimes eggs. Golden and silver eggs are hidden in some locations — explore thoroughly.

    First Mistakes to Avoid

    1. Skipping the demo — it's free and teaches you the core loop. 30 minutes in the demo prevents 2 hours of confusion in the full game.
    2. Ignoring dodge/parry — this is an action game. You cannot facetank everything. Learn the evade timings early.
    3. Rushing evolution without understanding — each evolution branch is a permanent choice. Read the tooltips before committing.
    4. Ignoring attributes — attributes directly affect combat performance. Don't put points into Essence on a physical brawler.
    5. Selling or discarding eggs — golden and silver eggs contain rare genes and variants. Keep them.
    6. Jumping straight to Abyss — Abyss is endgame content. Build a roster of evolved Voidlings first.
    7. Assuming all online info is accurate — the game launched yesterday. Most "guides" are pre-release speculation. Verify in-game.
